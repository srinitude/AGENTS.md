All written output must follow a structured drafting process. No final text may be produced without first completing purpose definition, audience identification, and outline construction. Every claim must be grounded. Every paragraph must earn its place.

---

## 1. Purpose Before Prose

* Every piece of writing must begin with an explicit purpose statement: what the reader should know, feel, or do after reading.
* The purpose statement must be written before any draft content.
* If the purpose cannot be stated in one or two sentences, the scope is too broad and must be narrowed.
* Writing without a declared purpose is prohibited.

---

## 2. Audience Identification

* The intended audience must be explicitly named before drafting begins.
* Assumptions about what the audience already knows must be stated, never implicit.
* Tone, vocabulary, and depth must be calibrated to the declared audience.
* If multiple audiences exist, each must be acknowledged and the primary audience chosen.

---

## 3. Outline First

* All writing must be outlined before any prose is drafted.
* The outline must reflect the purpose statement and audience.
* Each outline entry must represent one distinct idea or argument.
* Drafting without an approved outline is prohibited.

---

## 4. One Idea Per Paragraph

* Every paragraph must contain exactly one core idea.
* The first sentence must state or clearly imply that idea.
* Remaining sentences must support, elaborate, or evidence that single idea.
* Paragraphs that mix multiple unrelated claims must be split immediately.

---

## 5. Claims Require Grounding

* Every factual claim must be attributed to a source, experience, or explicit reasoning.
* Unsupported assertions are prohibited.
* If a source cannot be provided, the claim must be framed as opinion, hypothesis, or assumption — never as fact.
* Fabricated citations or hallucinated references are strictly forbidden.

---

## 6. Revision Process

* All writing must go through at least one revision pass after the initial draft.
* The revision pass must evaluate against the original purpose statement and audience.
* Each revision must check for: clarity, redundancy, unsupported claims, tone consistency, and structural coherence.
* Publishing or delivering a first draft as final output is prohibited.

---

## 7. Concision

* Every sentence must justify its existence relative to the purpose.
* Filler, throat-clearing, and generic preamble must be removed.
* Adverbs and qualifiers must be used sparingly and only when they add meaning.
* If a sentence can be cut without losing the paragraph's point, it must be cut.

---

## 8. Structural Clarity

* Headings must accurately describe the content that follows.
* Transitions between sections must be logical and explicit.
* Lists must be used for parallel items; prose must be used for connected reasoning.
* The reader must never have to guess why a section exists or what it contributes.

---

## 9. Voice and Consistency

* A consistent voice must be established at the start and maintained throughout.
* Shifts in tone, formality, or person must be intentional and acknowledged.
* Passive voice is permitted only when the actor is genuinely unknown or irrelevant.
* The default must always be active, direct construction.

---

## 10. Enforcement

* These are hard constraints, not style preferences.
* Any violation must be resolved before the writing is considered complete.
* No exceptions for "creative freedom" that bypass grounding, structure, or audience requirements.
* Writing that violates these constraints is considered incomplete and must not be delivered.
